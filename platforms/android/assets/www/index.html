  • Dual Balance Tanks With Product Sump

    JCS is sensitive to our Customer’s cost of operations for UHT and ESL products. That is why we have designed our systems to include a dual balance Tank arrangement, with the Product tank having a small narrow sump, which will assure a net positive suction to the feed pump while having a small volume to minimize the Product-Change-Over Losses. JCS typical losses on our large 125GPM UltraSys™, UltraFlex™, and all other UltraProcessors™ are in the area of 12 to 30 Gallons, while our Competitors lose 150 to 500 Gallons of the End User’s Product per Change-Over.

  • Flow Plate for CIP vs Production Configurations

    To facilitate a positive isolation between Modes of Operation (CIP & Process), we utilize a Flow Plate. This gives us a safe path for the Closed Loop Pressurized above Steam Table Temperature of Sterilization.

  • Product to Water to Product Indirect Regeneration

    When any of the Tubular Technologies JCS utilized Indirect Regeneration with Water as the media. One advantage to this is that the media will always be at a lower pressure than the Product on both the Raw and Pasteurized Sides of the heat exchangers.

  • Cascade Product to Hot Water Control

    JCS is always looking for the most efficient, and stable yet responsive means of controlling the process. By utilizing Cascade Control the process is typically five times more responsive to load changes which allows for tighter product temperature control.

  • Advanced Injector Control

    JCS utilizes a proprietary control method and set of JCS developed algorithms to control the Steam Flow to the Injector for ultimate stability and product quality. Our competitors typically control the Injector Valve Position by a simple Hold Tube Temperature Measurement. At JCS, we calculate the actual flow rate of steam that will be required to raise the temperature of the product feed to the injector to the desired Hold Tube Temperature. This JCS algorithm utilizes the following process parameters; Injector Inlet Temperature, Hold Tube Temperature Setpoint, Actual Hold Tube Temperature Measurement, Product Flow Rate in lb per min., Process Lead and Lag Times, Product Specific Density, Product Cp (Thermal Heat Capacity), Steam Pressure, Steam Temperature, Steam Flow, Metered Quantities for Changeovers to facilitate smooth transition in Interface of Changeover, and a feedback trim control to continuously tune the algorithm.

  • Advanced Temperature Ratio Control for Flash Cooling to Proper Solids Levels

    The Temperature of the Product out of the Flash Chamber is controlled to the same value as the Temperature going into the Injector. This assures that the Vapor removed from the product is equal to the Steam that was condensed into the Product in the Injector, and thus the density, % Solids, and % Fat will be identical to that of the injector feed. The Steam Table Temperature for the Product Temperature control assures accurate product consistency. The Pressure in the Flash Chamber is at below atmosphere to the pressure of steam at 170°F. This is accomplished as a cascade control of Temperature Ratio to Flash Chamber Vacuum.

  • Advanced Flash Chamber Vacuum Control

    The Flash Chamber Pressure is controlled to the Steam Table Temperature Pressure relationship for the desired product outlet temperature. The Vacuum is accomplished by collapsing the vapor that has flashed off the product, condensing the vapor on the surface area of the condenser. The level of condensation is proportional to the temperature on the media side of the condenser heat exchanger surface. This is controlled by modulating the flow of tower water through the exchanger. That flow is cascaded to an air break valve position controller, which is being modulated for the five to one more responsive measurement of Vacuum against the Vacuum pump. Thus the Vacuum pump is responsible for 10% of the Vacuum, while the Tower Water Flow through the Condenser is responsible for 90%.

  • Flow Plate Selection for Flex Between Direct and Indirect Production

    The UltraFlex™ can be configured to run in the Direct Steam Injection Mode as an UltraSys™ or in the Indirect Heating Mode as an UltraSpiral™ simply by changing the Swing Bend Connections on the Flow Plate. The proper hook-up is detected by means of proximity switch feedback.

  • Sterile Air Barrier on Flash Chamber Connections

    The UltraFlash™ is equipped with a Sterile Air Filter, which is sterilized with steam during the Sterilization of the UltraProcessor™. This provides Sterile Air for a Sterile Air Barrier to be placed on the Manway Seal, and clamp connections of the UltraFlash™, to prevent product burn-on that exists when the barrier is Steam, especially on the Door Housing.

  • Flash Chamber Level Control

    The UltraFlash™ Level is maintained in the outlet pipe leg, not in the vessel. This assures that no interface is created by the UltraFlash™ Level during product Change Overs. The Level is controlled by modulation of the Homogenizer speed.

  • Homogenizer Suction Pressure Control

    The Homogenizer follows the Flow Plate Configuration of the UltraFlex™.

    When in the Direct UltraSys™ mode, the Homogenizer is in the Pasteurized Zone downstream of the UltraFlash™.

    When in the Indirect UltraSpiral™ mode, the Homogenizer is in the Raw Zone and creates the higher pressure required to push the product through the Heat Exchangers.

  • Homogenizer Suction Pressure Control

    The Homogenizer follows the Flow Plate Configuration of the UltraFlex™.

    When in the Direct UltraSys™ mode, the Homogenizer is in the Pasteurized Zone downstream of the UltraFlash™.

    When in the Indirect UltraSpiral™ mode, the Homogenizer is in the Raw Zone and creates the higher pressure required to push the product through the Heat Exchangers.

  • Flexible Heat/Cooling on Exchangers Accelerates Sterilization and CIP Cycles

    The UltraFlex™ is equipped with routing valves on the Media Side of the Heat Exchangers which turns the Regenerator, and Cooler Sections into Heaters during the Sterilization Cycle, and back into Regenerators, and Coolers once the system has timed out the 30 minute sterilization timer and entered the UltraFlex™ Cool Down Cycle.

  • Cooler Control Strategy

    JCS is always looking for the most efficient and stable yet responsive means of controlling the process. By utilizing Cascade Control, the process is typically five times more responsive to load changes which allows for tighter product temperature control. The Cooling Loop is also equipped with a recirculation pump to assure a proper velocity of media in the heat exchanger. The Control strategy is one of feed and bleed of cooling media under temperature control of the circulated media, which is cascaded to the product outlet temperature.

  • Spiral Tube

    The Spiral Wound Tube in Shell Heat Exchangers are comprised of a coiled tube inside a media shell. The product flows through the tube, usually at high velocities. This design can accommodate viscous products as well as non-viscous, with or without particulates where high pressure capabilities may be required. Since there is only one product path, and that in a small high pressure tube, it eliminates the problem of fouling experienced in multi tubes. It is suited for higher pressure product. We have applied these to Aseptic Cream, Smoothies, Sauces, and Purees.
